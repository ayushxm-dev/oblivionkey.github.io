UMPC: Unlinkable Multi-Party Computation via Session-Specific Pseudonyms

Submitted

Paper submitted to Communications in Cryptology Vol. 3 Issue 1 (cic2026_1).

Unlinkable MPC (UMPC) adds anonymous credentials and session-specific pseudonyms to MPC to prevent cross-session linking. Supports classical (PS/BLS12-381) and post-quantum (ML-DSA) variants. Modular layer, compatible with existing MPC, and scales linearly.

  • Anonymous credentials and session-specific pseudonyms; classical (BLS12-381) and post-quantum (ML-DSA) variants
  • Modular layer, compatible with existing MPC; scales linearly with participant count

Tech: Go, BLS12-381, ML-DSA, ZK-SNARKs (Groth16)

Post-Quantum Selective Verifiability: Scaling Trust, Not Proofs

Submitted

Paper submitted to APKC 2026 (13th ACM ASIA Public-Key Cryptography Workshop).

Selective verifiability of computation DAGs: the verifier checks only a subset of nodes (vertex cut) instead of the full execution trace. Verified subset intersects every input–output path; nodes in subset proved with STARKs, rest authenticated via chained KMAC256. Subset selection uses entropy-regularised sampling and deterministic patching.

  • Proofs: STARK (FRI-based). Commitments: KMAC256, SHAKE256. HE: BFV, CKKS (Ring-LWE)
  • SHA-3-256, ML-DSA; Freivalds for matrix verification; PatchCut for subset selection

Tech: Rust, STARK, KMAC256, BFV/CKKS, ML-DSA

Underwater Quantum Key Distribution Simulator

Active

Simulation framework for quantum cryptography protocols in underwater environments using IBM Qiskit for quantum circuit modeling. Simulates QKD through underwater channels with realistic physical effects: photon absorption, scattering, thermal decoherence, and environmental noise.

Implemented protocols

  • BB84: Single-photon prepare-and-measure; two measurement bases (rectilinear and diagonal); security from quantum uncertainty
  • E91: Entanglement-based QKD using Bell states; CHSH inequality for eavesdropping detection; quantum correlation verification
  • GG02: Continuous-variable QKD; Gaussian-modulated coherent states; mutual information and Holevo bound

Tech: Python 3.7+, NumPy, Matplotlib, Qiskit, Qiskit Aer, pytest, tqdm, PyYAML

Underwater Quantum Clock Research System (Toolkit)

Completed

Simulation platform for analyzing quantum timing systems in harsh underwater environments: clock behavior, environmental adaptation, and adaptive control without hardware deployments.

Features

  • Multiple clock types: optical lattice, ion trap, Cs fountain, hydrogen maser
  • Real quantum mechanics: QuTiP-based quantum state evolution
  • Environmental adaptation: temperature, pressure, humidity, magnetic field compensation
  • Underwater environment: depth, salinity, turbidity, pH, water current; advanced noise (scattering, salinity, EM, vibration)
  • Digital twin: real-time sync and predictive modeling; adaptive control (ML-based)
  • Data export: JSON, CSV, HDF5 with underwater parameter tracking

Experiments

  • Thermocline test: temperature drop (0–100 m depth)
  • Deep ocean test: pressure effects (0–1000 m depth)
  • Extended duration test: long-term stability

Tech: Python, NumPy, SciPy, QuTiP, Plotly, Pandas, scikit-learn
